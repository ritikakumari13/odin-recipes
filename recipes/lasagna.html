Description

This vegetarian lasagna is a comforting and flavorful dish packed with layers of tender lasagna noodles, creamy ricotta cheese, sautéed mushrooms, spinach, and a rich marinara sauce. Topped with a blend of melted mozzarella and pecorino cheese, this lasagna is perfect for a hearty meal that satisfies without the need for meat. The combination of roasted vegetables and fresh herbs creates a depth of flavor that makes this dish a favorite among vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike.

Whether you're preparing it for a family dinner or a special gathering, this lasagna is sure to impress. The balance of textures and flavors ensures every bite is delicious, and the recipe is simple enough to follow while still delivering a gourmet experience.

Ingredients

  • 15 lasagna noodles
  • 3 cups marinara sauce
  • 3 cups fresh spinach
  • 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • ½ medium yellow onion, chopped
  • 3 cups whole milk ricotta cheese
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 teaspoons lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated pecorino cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley or basil for garnish

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Oil a 9×13-inch baking dish.
  2. Roast the vegetables: Place the mushrooms, red bell pepper, zucchini, and onion on the baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with salt and pepper, and toss to coat. Roast for 20-25 minutes until tender and browned around the edges. Reduce the oven temperature to 400°F (200°C).
  3. Cook the noodles: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and toss with a drizzle of olive oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Prepare the ricotta filling: In a large bowl, mix the ricotta cheese, garlic, lemon zest, salt, and black pepper.
  5. Assemble the lasagna: Spread 1 cup of marinara sauce at the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Add a layer of noodles, followed by half the ricotta mixture. Spread evenly, then arrange half the spinach on top. Add half the roasted vegetables and dot with ⅔ cup of marinara sauce.
  6. Repeat the layering: Add another layer of noodles, followed by the remaining ricotta, spinach, vegetables, and another ⅔ cup of sauce. Top with the final layer of noodles.
  7. Add the cheese topping: Spread the remaining ⅔ cup marinara over the pasta, then evenly sprinkle with mozzarella and pecorino cheese.
  8. Bake: Place the lasagna in the oven and bake for 30 minutes, or until the cheese is browned and bubbling.
  9. Rest and serve: Let the lasagna stand for 20 minutes before garnishing with fresh basil or parsley, slicing, and serving.
